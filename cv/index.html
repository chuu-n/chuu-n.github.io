Research

  • 2022.08 - 2026.07
    The Memory and Aging Lab at The University of Texas at Austin
    Research Associate
    Dr. Audrey Duarte
    In the Duarte lab, I worked on a project examining the relationship between sleep variability and episodic memory consolidation across the adult lifespan. I assisted in collecting EEG and fMRI data from participants aged 18 to 80 and administered behavioral assessments, including the Trail Making Test (TMT), Digit Span Memory Test, and California Verbal Learning Test (CVLT). I conducted principal component analysis in RStudio using sleep data gathered from wearable monitors and EEG data, which was preprocessed using EEGLAB in MATLAB. Through linear regression analyses, I explored how sleep variability relates to age and memory reconstruction. I presented this work at the SLEEP 2024 Conference in Houston and the Cognitive Neuroscience Society Conference in Boston, and I am currently preparing the manuscript for journal publication.
    • Aging, Memory, Executive Functioning, Cognitive Development
  • 2023.06 - 2025.07
    The Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience Lab at The University of Texas at Austin
    Research Associate (Lab manager)
    Dr. Jessica Church-Lang
    I oversaw participant recruitment, IRB protocols, and data management using the REDCap database. I conducted behavioral and fMRI data collection sessions using PsychoPy and am certified to operate Siemens 3T Vida MRI scanners.   • 2023.01 - 2023.04
    Neurological Genetic Disease Lab Course at The University of Texas at Austin
    Undergraduate Student
    Dr. Jonathan Pierce
    Worked under the supervision of Dr. Jonathan Pierce to investigate the genetic underpinnings of neurological disorders using C. elegans as a model organism. Induced environmental stressors to examine stress-related changes in fertility and egg-laying behaviors. Findings demonstrated a significant reduction in fertility rates among stress-susceptible C. elegans, contributing to a better understanding of how genetic vulnerability may influence stress responses in neurological conditions.
